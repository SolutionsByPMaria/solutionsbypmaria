📋 General Notarization

General notarization is the cornerstone of our services. As a state-commissioned notary public in Oklahoma, Princess Maria verifies identities, witnesses document signings, and places her official seal to authenticate documents — providing the layer of trust that courts, banks, and institutions require.

What is general notarization and why do I need it?

Notarization is the official process by which a notary public verifies your identity, witnesses you sign a document, and stamps it with an official seal to confirm its authenticity. It's required for many legal, financial, and personal documents to be recognized by courts, government agencies, banks, and other institutions. Without notarization, many important documents may be rejected or considered invalid.

What documents commonly require notarization?

Many documents require notarization, including: affidavits and sworn statements, powers of attorney, financial agreements, vehicle title transfers, child travel consent forms, medical authorization letters, business contracts, immigration documents, and more. If you're unsure whether your document needs notarization, contact us and we'll let you know right away.

How long does a notarization appointment take?

Most standard notarizations are completed in 15–30 minutes. More complex signings involving multiple documents or multiple signers may take longer. We recommend arriving or scheduling with all required parties present and all documents ready — but unsigned, as documents must be signed in the notary's presence.

What ID do I need to bring to my notarization?

You must bring a valid, current government-issued photo ID. Acceptable forms include: a state driver's license, state ID card, U.S. passport, military ID, or permanent resident card. Expired IDs cannot be accepted. All parties required to sign must present their own valid ID at the time of the appointment.

What is a mobile notary and how is it different from a regular notary?

A mobile notary is a notary public who travels to the client's location instead of requiring the client to visit a fixed office. This service is especially valuable for clients who are elderly, hospitalized, homebound, have tight schedules, or simply prefer the convenience of in-person service at their chosen location. All the same legal standards and requirements apply — the only difference is we come to you.

What does a loan signing agent do?

A loan signing agent is a specialized notary who guides borrowers through the signing of mortgage or refinance loan documents. We explain each document's general purpose (without providing legal advice), ensure all required signatures and initials are completed correctly, witness the signing, and return the completed package to the title company or lender. Our role is to make the closing process smooth, professional, and stress-free.

Are you certified as a loan signing agent?

Yes. In addition to my Oklahoma notary commission, I have completed professional loan signing agent training. I work with title companies, escrow firms, and signing services across the region. I carry Errors & Omissions (E&O) insurance for added protection and professionalism during every loan closing.

How do I schedule a loan signing appointment?

Loan signing appointments are typically scheduled through a title company, escrow officer, or signing service platform. If you're a title company or lender looking for a reliable signing agent in the Oklahoma City metro area, you can contact us directly to be added to our availability roster. Borrowers should coordinate through their lender or title company to request us by name.

What happens if there is an error in the loan documents?

Our job is to catch incomplete or inconsistent signatures and initials — not to review the legal terms of the loan. If we notice a document issue during the signing, we will flag it immediately and contact the title company or lender for guidance. We do not provide legal advice or alter any documents. Accuracy and attention to detail are our top priorities at every closing.

Do all parties need to be present for real estate document notarization?

Yes — any party whose signature requires notarization must appear in person before the notary with valid photo ID. Documents cannot be notarized by proxy or based on someone else's representation. Can you notarize real estate documents for out-of-state properties?

Yes! Oklahoma notaries can notarize documents that will be used in other states, as long as the signing takes place in Oklahoma and all standard notarization requirements are met. 📜 Estate & Will Documents

End-of-life planning documents are among the most sensitive and important papers a person can sign. Solutions by P Maria handles estate document notarizations with the utmost care, confidentiality, and professionalism — ensuring your wishes are properly documented and legally witnessed.

What estate documents can you notarize?

We provide notarization for a range of estate planning documents including: last will and testaments (where notarization is required), durable powers of attorney, healthcare powers of attorney and medical directives, living wills and advance directives, trust certifications, and personal property memorandums. Note: We are not attorneys and do not draft these documents — we notarize documents that have already been prepared, ideally by a licensed estate planning attorney.

Does a will need to be notarized in Oklahoma?

In Oklahoma, a will does not strictly require notarization to be valid — it requires two witnesses. However, a "self-proving affidavit" attached to a will does require notarization. This affidavit allows the will to be admitted to probate without the witnesses needing to testify in court. We strongly recommend having estate documents notarized for added legal protection and ease of probate.

Can you come to a hospital or home for estate document signings?

Yes, absolutely. We provide compassionate mobile notary services for estate and end-of-life documents at hospitals, hospice facilities, nursing homes, and private residences. We understand these situations require sensitivity and discretion, and we approach every appointment with the care and professionalism the moment deserves.

What is a power of attorney and do you notarize them?

A power of attorney (POA) is a legal document that grants one person (the agent) the authority to act on behalf of another (the principal) in financial, medical, or legal matters. Durable powers of attorney in Oklahoma must be notarized to be legally effective. We notarize all types of POA documents — general, durable, limited, and healthcare — for individuals and families across the Spencer area.

🌐 Apostille Services

Need a document authenticated for use in another country? An apostille is a specialized certificate issued by a government authority that verifies the authenticity of an official document for international use. Solutions by P Maria assists clients in understanding and navigating the apostille process with confidence.

What is an apostille and when do I need one?

An apostille is an official authentication certificate attached to a public document (such as a birth certificate, marriage certificate, diploma, or notarized document) that makes it legally recognized in countries that are members of the Hague Convention. You may need an apostille if you're working abroad, getting married in a foreign country, adopting internationally, pursuing foreign education, or conducting international business.

Can you provide apostille services directly?

As a notary, Princess Maria can notarize documents that are then submitted for apostille certification. The actual apostille certificate is issued by the Oklahoma Secretary of State (for state documents) or the U.S. Department of State (for federal documents). We assist by ensuring your document is properly notarized and prepared so the apostille application process goes smoothly.

What documents can receive an apostille?

Documents commonly submitted for apostille include: birth and death certificates, marriage and divorce certificates, adoption papers, educational diplomas and transcripts, notarized affidavits, corporate documents, and court documents. The document must first be an official or notarized document before it can receive an apostille stamp.

How long does the apostille process take?

The timeline varies. After notarization, submission to the Oklahoma Secretary of State for apostille typically takes 5–10 business days by mail, or same-day/next-day for in-person filing. Federal apostilles through the U.S. Department of State can take several weeks. We can help you understand the timeline and ensure your documents are prepared correctly to avoid delays.
